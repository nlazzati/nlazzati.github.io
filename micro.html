Course Description: The purpose of the course is to provide a self-contained analysis of the main building blocks of microeconomic theory, along with an introduction to some relevant mathematical notions such as concavity and supermodularity.

Syllabus: PDF

List of Topics:

  • Consumer theory (Ch. 1 and Section 2.3 of Jehle and Reny)
  • Theory of the firm (Ch. 3 of Jehle and Reny)
  • Decision under uncertainty and risk aversion (Section 2.4 of Jehle and Reny)
  • Partial equilibrium theory (Ch. 4 of Jehle and Reny)

There will be a methodological dimension to this course, with a self-contained introduction to

  • Convex analysis
  • General micro tools: envelope theorem, classical comparative statics, etc.
  • Supermodularity and comparative statics, with various economic applications

The above topics are all standard for a first course in microeconomics, except for the last item, a novel topic that has appeared in economic theory only recently.
